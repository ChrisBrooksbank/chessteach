# Kasparov vs Topalov, Wijk aan Zee 1999

Widely considered Kasparov's greatest game — and one of the finest attacking games of the modern era. A stunning rook sacrifice launches a king hunt that drags the Black king from g8 all the way across the board.

**Opening:** [Pirc Defense](../openings/semi-open/pirc-modern.md)

---

## The Game

```
1.e4 d6 2.d4 Nf6 3.Nc3 g6 4.Be3 Bg7 5.Qd2 c6 6.f3 b5 7.Nge2 Nbd7
8.Bh6 Bxh6 9.Qxh6 Bb7 10.a3 e5 11.O-O-O Qe7 12.Kb1 a6
13.Nc1 O-O-O 14.Nb3 exd4 15.Rxd4 c5 16.Rd1 Nb6 17.g3 Kb8
18.Na5 Ba8 19.Bh3 d5 20.Qf4+ Ka7 21.Re1 d4 22.Nd5 Nbxd5
23.exd5 Qd6 24.Rxd4!! cxd4 25.Re7+! Kb6 26.Qxd4+ Kxa5
27.b4+! Ka4 28.Qc3! Qxd5 29.Ra7 Bb7 30.Rxb7 Qc4
31.Qxf6 Kxa3 32.Qxa6+ Kxb4 33.c3+! Kxc3 34.Qa1+ Kd2
35.Qb2+ Kd1 36.Bf1! Rd2 37.Rd7! Rxd7 38.Bxc4 bxc4
39.Qxh8 Rd3 40.Qa8 c3 41.Qa4+ Ke1 42.f4 f5 43.Kc1 Rd2
44.Qa7 1-0
```

## Key Moments

### Position before 24.Rxd4!! — The rook sacrifice that launches the king hunt

Black has a passed d-pawn on d4, but Kasparov's pieces are poised for a devastating strike. The Na5 controls key squares, the Bh3 eyes the kingside, and both rooks are ready to explode into action.

After 24.Rxd4!! cxd4 25.Re7+! Kb6 26.Qxd4+ Kxa5 27.b4+! Ka4 — the Black king is dragged from a7 all the way to a4, deep in White's territory, with no hope of survival.

---

### 24.Rxd4!! — The rook sacrifice

The move that defines the game. Kasparov sacrifices the rook to tear open the position. The resulting attack is calculated to extraordinary depth.

### 25.Re7+! — The king hunt begins

After Kb6 26.Qxd4+ Kxa5 27.b4+! Ka4 — the Black king is dragged up the board, marching through hostile territory from g8 to a4!

### 28.Qc3! — Quiet brilliance

Not a check, not a capture — just a quiet queen move that creates unstoppable threats. This required incredible calculation from Kasparov.

## Lessons

1. **Romantic chess is alive** in the modern era — see [Tactics — Sacrifices](../tactics/sacrifices.md)
2. **Deep calculation** can produce brilliancies even against top opposition
3. **The king hunt** — when the king is exposed, every piece contributes to the attack
4. **Quiet moves** in the middle of a combination can be the strongest — see [The Evergreen Game](evergreen-game.md) for a similar lesson
