# Réti Opening

**1.Nf3 d5 2.c4**

Named after Richard Réti, the great hypermodern player. White attacks the centre from the flanks, often with g3 + Bg2 and c4, without playing d4 or e4 early. Extremely flexible.

**See also:** [English Opening](english.md) | [Catalan](../closed-games/catalan.md) | [King's Indian Attack](kings-indian-attack.md)

---

## Main Line

```
1.Nf3 d5 2.c4 d4 (or 2...c6, 2...e6) 3.b4 (or 3.e3, 3.g3)
```

### If 2...d4 (Black grabs space)
White can play 3.b4 (gambit for queenside development), 3.e3 (undermining), or 3.g3 (long-term pressure on d5).

### If 2...c6 or 2...e6 (Black holds)
White fianchettoes with g3 + Bg2 and may transpose to a [Catalan](../closed-games/catalan.md) or [English](english.md).

### Strategic Ideas

| White | Black |
|-------|-------|
| Hypermodern: control the centre from a distance | Occupy the centre with ...d5, ...e5 |
| Bg2 applies long-range pressure | Must decide: hold d5 or advance |
| Can transpose to many systems | Classical central play |
| Extreme flexibility | Solid development |

---

## Famous Practitioners

Richard Réti, Vladimir Kramnik, Magnus Carlsen.

## Who Should Play It

Players who enjoy fluid, positional play without committing early. The Réti is a universal system that can transpose into many openings depending on Black's response.
