# King's Indian Attack (KIA)

**1.Nf3, 2.g3, 3.Bg2, 4.d3, 5.Nbd2, 6.O-O, 7.e4**

A "system" opening — White plays the same setup regardless of Black's response. It's a reversed [King's Indian Defense](../indian-defenses/kings-indian.md) structure, and can be used against many Black setups (especially the [French](../semi-open/french-defense.md) and [Sicilian](../semi-open/sicilian-defense.md) move orders).

## Typical Setup

```
1.Nf3 d5 2.g3 Nf6 (or ...c5, ...e6) 3.Bg2 e6 4.O-O Be7 5.d3 O-O 6.Nbd2 c5 7.e4
```

### Strategic Ideas

| White | Black |
|-------|-------|
| Kingside attack: e4–e5, followed by Nf1–h2 or f4 | Central play: ...d5–d4 to seize space |
| The e5 advance cramps Black | Queenside expansion: ...b5, ...a5 |
| Bg2 supports the centre and kingside | Challenge the centre before White's attack develops |
| f4–f5 break for a direct attack | ...Nc6, ...Bd7, ...Rc8 — solid development |

### Common Plans

1. **e4–e5:** The key pawn advance. After e5, the kingside attack begins.
2. **Nf1–h2–g4 (or Nf1–e3–g4):** Knight manoeuvres to support the attack.
3. **f4–f5:** Open lines against the castled king.
4. **h4–h5:** In some positions, a direct kingside pawn storm.

---

## KIA vs French Structure

Particularly effective against setups with ...e6 and ...d5 — essentially a reversed [French Defense](../semi-open/french-defense.md) where White has an extra tempo.

## KIA vs Sicilian Structure

Against ...c5, ...Nc6, ...d6, White plays e4 and builds a [King's Indian](../indian-defenses/kings-indian.md) in reverse.

---

## Famous Practitioners

Bobby Fischer (used it frequently in the 1960s), Lev Psakhis, Vasily Smyslov.

## Who Should Play It

Players who want a universal system with a clear kingside attacking plan. The KIA requires understanding of attacking patterns rather than memorisation. Excellent for club players.
