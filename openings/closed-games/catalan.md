# Catalan Opening

**1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 e6 3.g3** (or 1.d4 d5 2.c4 e6 3.Nf3 Nf6 4.g3)

White combines the Queen's Gambit pawn structure (d4 + c4) with a fianchettoed bishop on g2. The Bg2 exerts tremendous long-range pressure on the centre and queenside along the a8–h1 diagonal. A favourite of world champions.

## Open Catalan (4...dxc4)

```
1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 e6 3.g3 d5 4.Bg2 dxc4 5.Nf3 Be7 6.O-O O-O 7.Qc2 a6 8.a4
```

Black captures on c4, accepting a pawn at the cost of development. White has long-term pressure with the Bg2 — the pawn often gets recovered naturally.

### Strategic Ideas

| White | Black |
|-------|-------|
| Bg2 pressure along the long diagonal | Hold the c4 pawn with ...b5 or return it for development |
| Often recover the pawn with superior piece play | ...a6 + ...b5 to hold the pawn is risky but possible |
| Qc2, a4 to restrain Black's queenside | Free piece development if the pawn is returned |

## Closed Catalan (4...Be7)

```
1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 e6 3.g3 d5 4.Bg2 Be7 5.Nf3 O-O 6.O-O Nbd7 7.Qc2 c6
```

Black maintains the central tension. The position becomes a strategic battle where White's Bg2 gives lasting pressure.

---

## Key Themes

1. **The Bg2:** The cornerstone of the Catalan. It dominates the long diagonal and targets a8, b7, c6, d5.
2. **Pawn recovery:** In the Open Catalan, White almost always recovers the c4 pawn while maintaining positional pressure.
3. **Endgame edge:** The Catalan often leads to positions where White has a small but persistent advantage.

## Famous Practitioners

Vladimir Kramnik (modern Catalan pioneer), Magnus Carlsen, Viswanathan Anand, Ding Liren.

## Who Should Play It

Positional players who enjoy long-term strategic pressure. The Catalan rewards patient play and deep understanding of piece activity.
